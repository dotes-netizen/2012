In addition to wines and liquors, the White Horse Farm’s specialties also include eggs. First up is the farm’s Red Soil Salted Duck Eggs. This product was originally designed to give visitors a DIY experience. However, the eggs were so tasty and such a big hit that many visitors requested to take boxes of them home. After fully covering fresh duck eggs with red soil and fine salt, let the eggs sit for about 20 days and they will be ready for eating. Different tastes will result when the soil-covered durations vary.
Packaged Red Soil Salted Eggs
After sitting for 20 days, the white material that has formed on the surface of the red soil is not mold, but is instead crystallized salt.
Wash away the red soil and it is finished.
The owner provided us with a simple way of the eggs boiled: place a few napkins under the eggs, water into the electric pot, boil and steam the eggs for a few minutes and the eggs will be ready.
The owner’s wife recommends the produced raw egg yolks to be used in making fried rice or stir-fry dishes, which will give your dishes exceptional flavor.
The cooked salted eggs with their golden egg yolk, which feels a little “sandy” when biting, have a taste which resembles that of the good old days.


Another type of egg product is preserved eggs made from chicken eggs. This product is the owner’s secret weapon and has not yet been publicly introduced. Because this special type of preserved egg is still under experimentation, it will only be officially introduced after its quality becomes fully stable. The preserved eggs are made by submerging the eggs into a sodium hydroxide solution under room temperature and allowing the protein in the eggs to condense into a gum-like texture after coming into contact with alkaline. In summer, this soaking process takes about 8 days, and then the eggs will be ready. The owner has the ability to judge whether or not the inside of the eggs have fully condensed by just tapping on the surface of the eggs and observing their vibrations.

Early methods of preserved egg production utilized heavy metals such as lead or copper sulfate, which was because people wanted to use those materials to clog up the pores on the egg shells to prevent the hydrogen sulfide inside the eggs from evaporating and resulting in the degradation of the egg compositions, and further causing unpleasant smells. However, nowadays, people are very conscious about eating natural and healthy foods, which is why the owner has developed new and healthy soaking solutions as well as vacuum packaging. In addition, the entire egg processing process is performed under refrigeration, allowing people to enjoy the excellent taste of preserved eggs and be healthy at the same time.
