How did your activities and research for this International Schools CyberFair project support content standards, required coursework and curriculum requirements?
 Through our participation in the Cyberfair project, we learned many skills that conform to the academic goals our school curriculum is designed to achieve. The following shows the skills that we learned as well as the area of study they fall under.
First, it is language. Through writing reports, doing interviews, producing progress reports, reading articles, as well as gathering, analyzing, and organizing data, we were able to further develop various language skills, including our writing and expression capabilities.
Second, it’s related to society. Through engagement in a project on a local topic, we were able to learn even more about the history, cultural background, and geographical characteristics of the area. In addition, we also gained further insight in to the area’s social transformations and changes in its economic activities over the years.
Third, through our on-site visits and photography of local scenery, we learned many photography and image placement techniques. In addition, through website and illustration production, we learned many techniques for artistic design.
Next, through team-based activities, we were all able to understand our individual capabilities and find our own sense of self-worth and value; we also got a chance to understand our own strengths and weaknesses, and we learned to appreciate other people’s strengths and advantages. We divided into teams to engage in interview activities, and each of the members in the team took turns in performing interviewing as well as recording, allowing each and every person to be able to clearly express their questions and ideas.
Last but not least, we learned many skills and knowledge in the area of information technology, including hardware skills, software skills, as well as techniques for performing Internet searching. In the end, we also produced a webpage to present the results of our entire project.

What information tools and technologies did you use to complete your Cyberfair project?
We used five information tools and technologies in total. They are video and digital still cameras (to took photographs as well as made videos.), video camera recorder (to recorded our interviews as well as our more dynamic activities.), computers (to search online for information, word processing, audio/video processing, and webpage production.), projectors (to play our audio/video recordings for discussion.), and printers (to print relative information).

In what ways did your students act as "ambassadors" and spokespersons for your Cyberfair project both on-line and in person.
In this Cyberfair project, we performed on-site visits and interviews, collected data, and continually contacted and talked to related people. We interacted with them in direct and indirect ways. For example, the owner was kind enough to provide everything we needed during the interview. Hence, we offered some feedback, and reflections for the owner. Also, the teacher helped him to cut several films. In this way, we were able to naturally play the role of an “ambassador.” We also hope that our webpage can allow even more people to learn more about the multi-theme farms, and attract even more people to visit it.

What has been or will be the impact of your project on your community?
After the interview, we and the owner of White Horse Farm got different ideas about our community. Besides, we found that many villagers don’t know there is such a pretty leisure farm in Hsienhsi due to lack of promotion. Fortunately, we did the project, and made this wonderful place known by more villagers. A few teachers in our school mentioned they would like to lead their students to do interview next time. Anyway, by this project, we hope it can bring our remote country, transforming industries, and immortal legend well-known around Taiwan, even the whole world.

How did your project involve other members of your community as helpers and volunteers?
The owner of White Horse Farm definitely helped us a lot. He provided everything we needed during the interview, including interview, DIY activities, pictures, and information. Concerning the DIY, the handicraft teacher of Lovely Moms’ Workroom also offered handicraft work for us. Besides, the horses keeper Mr. Huang did those like the owner of White Horse Farm, as well. He was so nice that we could experience horse riding. In addition, it should be contributed to the officer in Shengang Township Office. He did make us have a better understanding of Shengang Township. Lastly, Thanks to the students’ parents, they invited interviewer and gave a ride for us!

Discoveries, Lessons and Surprises
After completing our visitation and research activities, we now have an even deeper understanding of the White Horse Winery. In addition to opening our eyes to knowledge regarding horses, the production methods for producing salted eggs, and various wine-making processes, we also saw how our home industries were struggling and ways in which companies in the industry are seeking to transform and break through their current situation.

Special thanks to the "White Horse Farm " to provide full support.
